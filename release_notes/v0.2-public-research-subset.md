## Release Summary

v0.2 marks the first public disclosure of research data from the Cinematic Emotion Lab
corpus. The annotation framework governing emotional ground truth in this program is now
publicly documented. A curated public research subset - acoustic feature vectors derived
from the annotated corpus - is available for community engagement and independent analysis.

This release answers a foundational question about the research: *is the dataset real,
structured, and governed by a defensible annotation methodology?*

The answer is documented in full.

---

## What Is Released

### 1. Annotation Schema — `datasets/annotations/schema.json`

The formal specification for the Cinematic Emotion Lab annotation framework is now public.
This is the complete structural definition of how emotional ground truth is encoded in this
research program: field definitions, ordinal scale specifications, edge case flag taxonomy,
annotation protocol metadata, and citation guidance.

The schema documents a methodologically distinctive design decision: **ground truth in
this research is established from compositional intent, not listener perception.** The
annotator is the composer of the material being annotated. The annotation target is what
was being constructed, not what audiences are expected to feel.

This distinction separates the dataset from standard music emotion recognition corpora
and has direct theoretical implications for how the trained models should be interpreted.
Full methodological justification is documented in `docs/METHODOLOGY.md`.

### 2. Feature Distribution Analysis — `NB-EDA-001`

`notebooks/exploratory/NB-EDA-001_feature-distributions_2026-08-01.ipynb`

An exploratory analysis of the acoustic feature space structure, operating exclusively
on the public corpus subset. The notebook characterizes:

- Per-dimension variance profiles across the 71-dimensional feature space
- Feature group distribution shapes: MFCCs, Chroma CQT, Spectral, RMS, ZCR, Tempo, H/P Ratio
- Feature correlation matrix with acoustic domain boundary annotations
- Feature distributions by `harmonic_mode` and `narrative_position` structural metadata
- MFCC mean coefficient corpus profile
- Corpus-level Chroma CQT radar visualization

The analysis is structural. It examines the geometry of the feature space without
reference to emotion labels, which are withheld from the public subset.

**Figures released:**
- `NB-EDA-001_feature-group-distributions.png` — violin plot suite across 7 acoustic domains
- `NB-EDA-001_variance-profile.png` — per-dimension variance across 71 features
- `NB-EDA-001_correlation-matrix.html` — interactive 71×71 Pearson correlation heatmap
- `NB-EDA-001_features-by-harmonic-mode.png` — feature distributions by harmonic mode
- `NB-EDA-001_features-by-narrative-position.html` — interactive box plots by narrative position
- `NB-EDA-001_mfcc-mean-profile.html` — interactive MFCC coefficient profile with std envelope
- `NB-EDA-001_chroma-radar.html` — interactive corpus Chroma CQT radar chart

## Public Corpus Subset

The public corpus subset is available at `datasets/processed/public_subset_features.csv`.

**Contains:**
- 71-dimensional acoustic feature vectors (one row per cinematic audio cue)
- Anonymized clip identifiers (`CEL-2026-XXXX` format)
- Structural metadata fields: `harmonic_mode`, `narrative_position`, `tempo_category`, `narrative_function`

**Does not contain:**
- Emotion labels (`primary_emotion`, `emotional_intensity` — withheld pending publication)
- Full annotation fields (`orchestration_density`, `secondary_emotion`, `annotation_confidence` — withheld)
- Composer notes
- Raw audio (not committed — IP protection)

## What This Release Signals

The research corpus is real. The annotation methodology is transparent and academically
defensible. The annotation protocol encodes compositional intent rather than listener
perception — a methodological distinction with direct theoretical consequences for how
the trained models should be interpreted and how the Composer Gap study (EXP-004)
should be read.

The corpus is structured, representative of the research program's full scope, and
larger than what is being shown here.
