## v0.1 - Foundation Layer
**Released: June 2026**
**GitHub Release Title:** `v0.1 - Foundation Layer: Research Infrastructure and Identity`

### Release Notes

The Cinematic Emotion Lab is publicly established.

This release discloses the complete research infrastructure: methodology documentation,
experimental framework, visual identity system, system architecture specification,
and the first formal experiment in the research pipeline.

**What is being released:**
- Full repository scaffold with research governance documents (ROADMAP.md,
  RESEARCH_STRATEGY.md, STAGED_RELEASE_STRATEGY.md, GIT_STANDARDS.md,
  NAMING_CONVENTIONS.md, CONTRIBUTING.md)
- Visual identity system: hero banner, pipeline diagram, emotion heatmap,
  research architecture graphic, spectrogram intelligence visualization
- `NB-EXP-001` - Full acoustic feature extraction pipeline (71-dimensional feature space)
- Research philosophy, methodology overview, and architecture documentation
- Composer vs. AI interpretation framework: theoretical framing for EXP-004
- Public positioning documentation and staged disclosure framework

**What this signals:**
This is a structured, institution-grade research program with a defined experimental
protocol, a unique interdisciplinary position, and a publication-track trajectory.

**What remains withheld:**
Classification results, trained model weights, annotated corpus, and quantitative
findings from EXP-002, EXP-003, and EXP-004 are embargoed pending publication.

## v0.25 - Annotation Intelligence Framework
**Target: September 2026**
**GitHub Release Title:** `v0.25 - Annotation Intelligence: Compositional Intent as Ground Truth`

### Release Notes

The theoretical and methodological architecture of the composer annotation system
is now fully public.

This release surfaces the most conceptually original component of this research:
the formal encoding of compositional intent as machine-readable ground truth.
This methodological decision - annotating from the composer's stated intent,
not from listener perception - is what separates this work from prior art in
music emotion recognition.

**What is being released:**
- Complete annotation schema documentation with per-field rationale
- Composer annotation protocol: decision trees, edge case taxonomy,
  inter-field consistency enforcement rules
- Sample annotated entries with composer reasoning (text only, audio withheld)
- `NB-EDA-002` - Annotation distribution analysis: intensity, narrative position,
  harmonic mode across the corpus
- Selected composer intuition log excerpts (curated, non-disclosing)
- Inter-annotator agreement design document

**What this signals:**
The ground truth in this research is not crowd-sourced perception - it is
professional compositional judgment, formally encoded. This is a first-class
methodological contribution.

**What remains withheld:**
Full annotation corpus, classification results, and quantitative characterization
of inter-annotator agreement are embargoed.

## v0.3 - Baseline Intelligence Results
**Target: October 2026**
**GitHub Release Title:** `v0.3 - Baseline Results: The Machine Has an Answer`

### Release Notes

The first generation of classification results from the Cinematic Emotion Lab
research pipeline is now publicly available.

Initial experiments establish that cinematic emotional categories exhibit
statistically significant separability within the 71-dimensional acoustic
feature space constructed by this research. The shape of these results - which
features carry the most signal, which emotion categories are most and least
separable - is disclosed in qualitative terms. Quantitative characterization
is withheld pending peer-reviewed publication.

**What is being released:**
- `NB-EXP-002` - Baseline emotion classification notebook
  (architecture and training procedure fully disclosed; per-class metrics withheld)
- Qualitative results framing: separability narrative and feature importance summary
- Feature importance analysis: relative acoustic domain rankings
- `NB-VIZ-003` - Feature space topology: UMAP projection of the emotion corpus
  (cluster structure visible; categorical labels withheld)
- Model configuration: `models/configs/EXP-002_config.yaml`
- Week 09-14 research logs

**What this signals:**
Emotion classification from acoustic features in the cinematic domain is
demonstrably feasible. The research has moved from theoretical framing into
empirical demonstration.

**What remains withheld:**
Per-class accuracy, F1 scores, confusion matrices, and absolute performance
benchmarks are embargoed.

## v0.35 - Harmonic Tension Intelligence
**Target: November 2026**
**GitHub Release Title:** `v0.35 - Harmonic Tension: The Machine Learns Emotional Trajectory`

### Release Notes

The temporal modeling layer of the Cinematic Emotion Lab is now publicly disclosed.

This release surfaces the transition from static emotion classification to dynamic,
sequence-aware harmonic tension prediction - a qualitatively different research
contribution from clip-level analysis. Early trajectory visualizations suggest
that harmonic motion in film music follows learnable arc patterns aligned to
narrative structure.

**What is being released:**
- `NB-EXP-003` - Harmonic tension sequence model: architecture and training
  protocol (evaluation metrics withheld)
- Harmonic tension trajectory visualizations for selected anonymized clips
- Temporal emotion arc analysis: emotional profile evolution across narrative positions
- Architecture comparison document: LSTM vs. Transformer approach rationale
- `models/configs/EXP-003_config.yaml`
- Week 15-18 research logs

**What this signals:**
The research has crossed a meaningful threshold: from recognizing static emotional
snapshots into modeling emotional motion through time. This is the experimental
foundation for the full publication narrative.

**What remains withheld:**
Model evaluation metrics, prediction accuracy over time, and trained sequence
model weights are embargoed.

## v0.4 - Interpretability and Divergence Layer
**Target: December 2026**
**GitHub Release Title:** `v0.4 - Composer Gap: The Architecture of Divergence`

### Release Notes

The Composer Gap study - the central theoretical contribution of the Cinematic
Emotion Lab - is partially disclosed.

The full methodology of EXP-004 is now public. A structured taxonomy of named
divergence failure modes is released. The architecture of where and how machine
perception diverges from composer-annotated intent is surfaced without quantitative
characterization. Quantitative divergence metrics remain embargoed.

**What is being released:**
- EXP-004 study design: complete methodology disclosure without quantitative results
- Divergence taxonomy: four named failure mode categories with qualitative description
- Selected annotation excerpts demonstrating divergence structure
- `NB-EXP-004` - Composer Gap analysis notebook (architecture and methodology visible;
  divergence metrics withheld)
- Interpretability framework: acoustic feature tracing for prediction error analysis
- Week 19-24 research logs

**What this signals:**
This research has moved beyond classification into interpretation. The Composer Gap
is not treated as error - it is the primary object of study, and its structure
is now publicly documented.

**What remains withheld:**
Quantitative divergence metrics, gap magnitude analysis, and full failure mode
taxonomy with prevalence statistics are embargoed pending publication.
