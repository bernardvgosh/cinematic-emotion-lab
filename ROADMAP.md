# Research Roadmap
## Cinematic Emotion Lab - Staged Public Disclosure Framework

> Each release represents a deliberate layer of cinematic emotion intelligence research
> being surfaced for public engagement. The sequence is designed to build intellectual
> momentum, sustain curiosity, and position this work at the frontier of interdisciplinary
> AI research before formal publication.

---

## Release Philosophy

This roadmap governs the staged public disclosure of an active, substantially complete
research program. Releases are sequenced to:

- Establish presence before revealing depth
- Surface methodology before surfacing results
- Build a public research record aligned to publication
- Create sustained engagement across AI, film, and music communities
- Demonstrate research acceleration through frequent, meaningful updates

Each release is a **research artifact** - not a notebook upload.
Each milestone advances the public understanding of cinematic emotion intelligence
by one deliberate layer.

## Release Sequence

### v0.1 - Foundation Layer
**Status: RELEASED - June 2026**
**Theme: Infrastructure and Identity**

The public research infrastructure is established. The visual identity, pipeline
architecture, methodology documentation, and foundational experiment are disclosed.

**What this release signals:**
This is a serious, structured research program with institution-grade documentation,
a defined experimental framework, and a unique interdisciplinary position.

**Public artifacts:**
- Full repository scaffold with research governance documents
- Visual identity system: hero banner, pipeline diagram, emotion heatmap,
  spectrogram intelligence graphic, research architecture visual
- `NB-EXP-001` - Acoustic feature extraction pipeline (71-dimensional feature space)
- Research philosophy, methodology overview, architecture documentation
- Composer vs. AI interpretation framework (EXP-004 conceptual framing)
- Staged disclosure strategy and research roadmap

### v0.15 - Visualization Intelligence Layer
**Status: UPCOMING - July 2026**
**Theme: The Research Has Eyes**

The first generation of research visualizations is disclosed. These are not
illustrative figures - they are analytical instruments that surface structure

**What this release signals:**
The research is producing analytical output. The feature space has visible structure.
There is something to see - and the most significant findings are still withheld.

**Public artifacts:**

- Waveform and beat-grid visualizations with narrative arc overlays
- MFCC coefficient heatmap comparison suite

- RMS dynamic envelope visualization per narrative position
- `NB-VIZ-001` - Spectrogram Intelligence notebook
- `NB-VIZ-002` - Chroma and harmonic profile visualization notebook
- Week 01-04 research logs

**Language to use publicly:**
> "Initial visualizations from the cinematic emotion feature space reveal structured

> its first research-grade outputs."

### v0.2 - Computational Annotation Preview
**Status: UPCOMING - August 2026**
**Theme: The Research Has Ground Truth**

A curated subset of the research corpus is disclosed alongside the annotation
framework that governs how emotional intent is formally encoded in this system.
This is the first public evidence that the dataset is real, structured, and
governed by a rigorous labeling protocol.

**What this release signals:**
The ground truth exists. The annotation methodology is transparent and defensible.
The corpus is larger and more structured than what is being shown.

**Public artifacts:**
- Curated public research subset: anonymized acoustic feature matrix
  (representative clips, emotional labels withheld pending publication)
- Annotation framework specification: `datasets/annotations/schema.json`
- Annotation protocol document: inter-annotator design, rationale, edge case handling
- `NB-EDA-001` - Feature distribution analysis across the disclosed corpus subset
- Preliminary corpus statistics: duration, tempo distribution, harmonic mode breakdown
- Week 05-08 research logs

**Language to use publicly:**
> "The annotation framework governing emotional ground truth in this research
> is now publicly documented. A curated subset of the acoustic feature corpus
> has been released for community engagement, with full labels held for publication."

### v0.25 - Annotation Intelligence Framework
**Status: UPCOMING - September 2026**
**Theme: The Composer Speaks**

The conceptual and methodological architecture of the composer annotation system
is disclosed in depth. This release surfaces the most theoretically original
component of the research - the formal encoding of compositional intent as
machine-readable ground truth.

**What this release signals:**
The ground truth in this research is not crowd-sourced perception - it is
professional compositional judgment, formally encoded. This is a methodological
distinction that separates this work from prior art in music emotion recognition.

**Public artifacts:**
- Full annotation schema documentation with field rationale
- Composer annotation protocol: decision trees, edge case taxonomy, inter-field
  consistency rules
- Sample annotated clips with composer reasoning (text only, audio withheld)
- `NB-EDA-002` - Annotation distribution analysis: emotional intensity, narrative
  position, harmonic mode across the corpus
- Composer intuition log excerpts (selected, non-disclosing entries from
  research-logs)
- Preliminary inter-annotator agreement design document

**Language to use publicly:**
> "The annotation architecture governing emotional ground truth in this system
> treats compositional intent - not listener perception - as the primary signal.
> The formal encoding of that intent is now publicly documented."

---

### v0.3 - Baseline Intelligence Results
**Status: UPCOMING - October 2026**
**Theme: The Machine Has an Answer**

The first generation of classification results is disclosed. The framing is
deliberately qualitative at this stage - demonstrating that the system works,
characterizing the shape of the results, and withholding per-class quantitative
breakdown pending publication.

**What this release signals:**
The research is producing results. Emotion classification from acoustic features
is demonstrably feasible in the cinematic domain. The most precise and
theoretically significant findings remain withheld.

**Public artifacts:**
- `NB-EXP-002` - Baseline emotion classification notebook
  (architecture visible, per-class accuracy withheld)
- Qualitative results framing: separability statement, feature importance narrative
- Feature importance analysis: which acoustic domains carry the most signal
  (relative rankings, not absolute scores)
- `NB-VIZ-003` - Feature space topology: UMAP projection of the emotion corpus
  (cluster structure visible, categorical labels withheld)
- Model configuration files: `models/configs/EXP-002_config.yaml`
- Week 09-14 research logs

**Language to use publicly:**
> "Initial classification experiments establish that cinematic emotional categories
> exhibit statistically significant separability within the acoustic feature space
> constructed by this research. Quantitative results are withheld pending
> peer-reviewed publication."

### v0.35 - Harmonic Tension Intelligence
**Status: UPCOMING - November 2026**
**Theme: The Machine Learns to Feel Tension**

The temporal modeling layer is disclosed. This release surfaces the most
technically sophisticated component of the system - the transition from static
emotion classification to dynamic, sequence-aware harmonic tension prediction.

**What this release signals:**
The research has moved beyond clip-level classification into temporal modeling
of emotional trajectory. This is a qualitatively different research contribution
from standard music emotion recognition.

**Public artifacts:**
- `NB-EXP-003` - Harmonic tension sequence model: architecture and training
  protocol disclosed, evaluation withheld
- Harmonic tension trajectory visualization for selected anonymized clips
- Temporal emotion arc analysis: how emotional profile evolves across narrative
  positions within a single cue
- LSTM/Transformer architecture comparison document
- `models/configs/EXP-003_config.yaml`
- Week 15-18 research logs

**Language to use publicly:**
> "The harmonic tension modeling system represents a transition from static
> emotion recognition to dynamic emotional trajectory analysis. Early trajectory
> visualizations suggest that harmonic motion in film music follows learnable
> arc patterns aligned to narrative structure."

---

### v0.4 - Interpretability and Divergence Layer
**Status: UPCOMING - December 2026**
**Theme: Where the Machine Fails to Hear**

The Composer Gap study is partially disclosed. This is the most intellectually
original and theoretically rich component of the research - the systematic
characterization of where and how machine perception diverges from compositional
intent. Quantitative findings are withheld; the architecture of the divergence
is surfaced.

**What this release signals:**
The research has moved from classification into interpretation. The gap between
computational perception and compositional intent is not being minimized - it is
being studied as the primary theoretical contribution.

**Public artifacts:**
- EXP-004 study design: full methodology disclosure without quantitative results
- Divergence taxonomy: named failure mode categories (qualitative, non-quantified)
- Selected composer annotation excerpts demonstrating the gap's structure
- `NB-EXP-004` - Composer Gap analysis notebook (architecture visible,
  divergence metrics withheld)
- Interpretability framework: how the system traces prediction errors back to
  specific acoustic feature combinations
- Week 19-24 research logs

**Language to use publicly:**
> "The Composer Gap study - a systematic comparison of machine predictions against
> composer-annotated intent - has produced a structured taxonomy of perception
> divergence. The architecture of this divergence is now publicly documented.
> Quantitative characterization is withheld pending publication."
