# Code of Conduct
## Cinematic Emotion Lab

---

## Purpose

The Cinematic Emotion Lab is a research repository engaging an interdisciplinary
community: AI and machine learning researchers, computational musicologists, film
composers, sound designers, music theorists, and others with substantive interest
in the intersection of audio signal processing and emotional intelligence in film.

This Code of Conduct defines the standards for all interactions within this
repository - Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, code review comments, and any
other form of participation. It exists to protect the integrity of a research
community that spans technical and creative disciplines and to ensure that
participation in this repository is substantive, respectful, and professionally
constructive.

---

## Standards

### Expected Conduct

- Engage with intellectual honesty. Critique ideas on their technical and
  theoretical merits.
- Maintain a professional research register in written contributions. This is a
  publication-track research repository, not a casual forum.
- Acknowledge uncertainty clearly. If you are speculating, say so.
- Respect disciplinary differences. Technical expertise in ML does not confer
  authority on compositional theory, and vice versa. Engage across disciplines
  with appropriate epistemic humility.
- Give credit where it is due. Cite sources. Attribute ideas correctly.
- Disagreement is expected and productive. Pursue it through argument,
  not dismissal.

### Unacceptable Conduct

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language in any form.
- Deliberate misrepresentation of the research, its methodology, or its findings.
- Attempts to extract, reconstruct, or publish embargoed research findings,
  datasets, or materials through any means.
- Submission of material that violates intellectual property rights — audio files,
  datasets, code, or documentation belonging to third parties without appropriate
  licensing.
- Sustained disruption of substantive discussion through repeated off-topic or
  low-quality participation.
- Unsolicited commercial outreach or promotional material in Issues or Discussions.

---

## Research Integrity

Participants in this repository are expected to observe standards consistent with
research integrity in their own contributions:

- Do not fabricate data, results, or analytical observations.
- Do not misrepresent your credentials, affiliation, or role in any interaction.
- Do not attribute ideas or findings to yourself that derive from others' work
  without acknowledgment.
- If you identify a potential methodological problem, error, or reproducibility
  concern in the public materials, raise it through an Issue. This is a valued
  contribution.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all repository spaces: Issues, Pull Requests,
GitHub Discussions, code review, and commit messages. It also applies to any
representation of participation in this project in external venues — conferences,
social media, academic publications — where conduct could affect the reputation
or integrity of the research program.

---

## Enforcement

The primary researcher (Bernard G.) is the sole maintainer of this repository
and is responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct.

**Procedure:**

1. Issues are raised by contacting the maintainer directly at
   contactbernardg@gmail.com with the subject line
   `[Cinematic Emotion Lab] Code of Conduct`.
2. All reports are reviewed privately and treated with discretion.
3. The maintainer will respond within seven business days.
4. Consequences for violations range from a written request to modify conduct,
   to removal of contributions, to permanent restriction from the repository,
   depending on severity and recurrence.

The maintainer is committed to applying these standards consistently and without
regard to the standing, technical reputation, or affiliation of the participant.
